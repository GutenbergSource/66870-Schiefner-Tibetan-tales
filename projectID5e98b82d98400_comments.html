This collection of Tibetan tales is translated from a German translation of a Tibetan book, the Kanjur, but is ultimately derived from Sanskrit originals, such as the Jataka or the Kathá Sarit Ságara. This work has all the hallmarks of a 19th century scientific work: lots and lots of footnotes, and many accented letters, such as macrons and under-dots. Please be aware of these accents, and encode them following the standard practices.

Have fun!

The original scans are at the Internet Archive.
